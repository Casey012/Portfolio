Sector Food Management and Sustainability
Objective To reduce food waste in households.
Solution The device helps reduce food waste by tracking expiration dates and suggesting recipes based on available ingredients, preventing spoilage.
Team Angelina Janicas, Kaya Kanashiro, Jasmine Gill, and Khaled Bleibleh.
My Role UX designer and Team Coordinator
Project Time 2 Months
Project Type School

Process

Introduction

For this project we had to design a product that revolved around a social issue. The fridge monitor is an innovative and sustainable solution to household food waste. The fridge monitor is a system that combines user-friendly technology and environmental awareness to reduce waste, promote sustainable food consumption, and help users save money. The monitor will utilize a scanning system placed on the fridge's exterior, allowing users to track the expiration dates of stored items. An integrated recipe feature will provide meal suggestions based on available ingredients, reducing the likelihood of spoilage.

Challenges and Constraints

  1. Simplicity: this device would be used by a wide range of users, and so we needed to keep the design simple. The design had to be intuitive for users of all ages and technical knowledge.
  2. Technical Challenges:Determining the most effective method to scan items and accurately track their expiration dates.
  3. Placement:We wanted the device to seamlessly integrate into users' routines rather than disrupt them, so we added additional scanners that could easily be incorporated into various fridge models.

Prototyping

During prototyping, we explored ideas for both the appearance and functionality of the device. This included determining the ideal screen size and incorporating additional sensors inside the fridge to simplify the scanning process. We also focused on designing the user interface (UI) of the screen to ensure it was intuitive and user-friendly.

Conclusion

Reflecting on this project, I gained valuable insights into the importance of user-centered design and the role of technology in addressing real-world issues like food waste. I learned how crucial it is to balance functionality with simplicity, ensuring that the device integrates smoothly into usersâ€™ daily routines without feeling like a burden. The technical challenges of scanning items and tracking expiration dates highlighted the need for thoughtful solutions and reliable technology. I also learned the significance of considering both the physical design and the user interface to create a cohesive, intuitive experience. Ultimately, this project taught me how to approach problem-solving creatively, while also considering the practical aspects of usability and sustainability. If we had the resources and time to continue, I would have tested a barcode scanner to assess its viability for tracking expiration dates. Additionally, I would have explored the optimal placement of scanners to ensure they fit seamlessly into users' spaces and lifestyles, ensuring the device functions effectively. Through this project, I also learned valuable lessons about collaboration and how to bring a team together to work efficiently toward a shared goal.
