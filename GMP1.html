Process

Introduction

For this project, we have chosen to design an accessible pill box that focuses on better medication management for users with diverse needs. Our focus is on elderly people who may struggle with memory or motor issues. Our box includes features like automatically dropping pills and reminders to encourage people to take their medication. We want to enhance users' experience with taking their medication on time with the proper dosage. By implementing these features, it will result in a more seamless experience for users and ensure that they live a healthier life. In out society were the population has an increasing amount of seniors, we must design with the intention to help their quality of life. My team decided to tackle the classic pill box and make into an automated device. First, we had to understand the challenges that seniors may face.

Challenges and Constraints

  1. Ensuring accessibility:we must make the device is useable with a variety of disabilities. Including limited vision, hearing, motor limitations and more.
  2. Technological difficulties:the elderly may not have a good grasp on new technology, and so we had to ensure that our device was user-friendly and simple to use.
  3. Ergonomics: we also had to consider how the device would fit into someone’s home, like the size it should be and decided whether it should be portable.

We then came up with this idea. The device would drop pills automatically and would sync to an app where customizations about time are made. The pills would be inserted into slots for each day of the week. We also added a reminder system so that the pills wouldn’t be forgotten by adding a weight sensor to the pill plate that would alert users after some time.

Prototyping

Our first prototype was a rough sketch of ideas. We then moved on to the physical prototype. We made this from cardboard, the main objective was to figure out the sizing, and how the pills would insert into the device. We created a slider system that where we could manually drop the pills.


After prototyping and gathering feedback, we learned some key points. The first was inserting the pills for each day caused an issue. If the user had to take multiple pills throughout the day the device wouldn’t be able to do that. Instead we changed it that each slot would be a different medication. With weight scales in each slot the device would drop the right amount of each pills.

Conclusion

Following this process, we began considering future possibilities for the device. For instance, selecting a high-contrast color scheme to ensure readability, making the design aesthetically pleasing to encourage users to incorporate it into their homes, and integrating features like braille to enhance accessibility for visually impaired individuals. However, because this was a school assignment, we lacked the time to conduct thorough research and user testing for our prototype. Ideally, I would have liked to perform interviews, usability testing, and A/B testing to refine the design and ensure it effectively meets user needs.
